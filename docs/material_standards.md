# NYC Street Design Manual Material Standards

## Overview

This guide documents the complete material taxonomy and standards system codified from the NYC Street Design Manual, federal ADA requirements, and industry standards. This system enables data-driven sidewalk maintenance, cost analysis, and regulatory compliance reporting.

**Key References:**
- [NYC Street Design Manual](https://www.nycstreetdesign.info/)
- [NYC Administrative Code Title 34 (DOT Rules)](https://www1.nyc.gov/assets/dot/downloads/pdf/sidewalk-inspection-manual.pdf)

- [ADA Accessibility Guidelines (28 CFR 36)](https://www.ada.gov/businesslaw/2010ADAstandards_index.html)

## Material Categories

### 1. Asphalt (ASPHALT)

**Standard Material:** Hot Mix Asphalt (HMA), 12.5mm SuperPave

**Description:** Most common NYC sidewalk surface. Cost-effective, proven performance in NYC climate.

**Specifications:**
- Thickness: 2 inches
- Compaction: 96%
- Binder Grade: PG58-28 (6% traffic lane, 28°C minimum)
- Slip Resistance: 65 BPN (British Pendulum Number)

**Maintenance Schedule:**
- Routine seal coat: Every 3 years
- Preventive overlay: Year 7
- Full reconstruction: Year 20

**Lifecycle Cost:** $12.00/sqft (including maintenance and replacement)

**ADA Compliance Requirements:**
- Width: Minimum 4 feet clear
- Slope: Max 5% longitudinal, 2% cross
- Slip resistance: Minimum 60 BPN
- Surface firmness: No holes > 0.25 inch
- Changes in level: Max 0.5 inch vertical

**Environmental Factors:**
- Freeze-thaw: Critical in NYC climate (requires seal coating)
- Salt exposure: High (seal coat extends life 5-7 years)
- UV degradation: Moderate (seal coat slows oxidation)

**Cost Analysis:**
- Installation: $2.50/sqft
- Lifecycle: $12.00/sqft
- Annual maintenance: ~0.40/sqft

### 2. Permeable Asphalt (ASPH_POROUS)

**Description:** Open-graded asphalt with 15-20% infiltration rate. Reduces stormwater runoff.

**Specifications:**
- Thickness: 2.5 inches
- Porosity: 18% air voids
- Infiltration: 360 in/hr (excellent)
- Slip Resistance: 65 BPN

**Maintenance Schedule:**
- Vacuum sweeping: Every 6 months (CRITICAL for function)
- Pressure washing: Every 2 years
- Overlay: Year 10 if infiltration drops below 85%

**Lifecycle Cost:** $18.50/sqft (higher due to frequent maintenance)

**Sustainability:** 78/100 (stormwater benefits, reduced runoff)

**Key Difference:** Requires much more frequent maintenance (clogging risk). Best for areas with strong maintenance programs.

### 3. Portland Cement Concrete (CONCRETE)

**Standard Material:** Air-entrained PCC, 4-inch jointed slabs

**Description:** Premium material with excellent durability. Very long service life (25-30 years).

**Specifications:**
- Thickness: 4 inches
- Compressive strength: 3,500 PSI
- Air content: 6% (freeze-thaw protection)
- Water-cement ratio: 0.45
- Joint spacing: 4 feet (doweled butt joints)
- Surface finish: Broom finish (slip resistance)

**Maintenance Schedule:**
- Joint sealing: Every 5 years
- Crack repair: Every 3 years as needed
- Spall repair: As needed (safety critical)
- Overlay: Year 15
- Full reconstruction: Year 30

**Lifecycle Cost:** $14.00/sqft (lower total cost despite higher initial cost)

**Benefits:**
- Longest lifespan (30 years)
- Minimal maintenance after initial period
- Excellent freeze-thaw resistance
- Good for high-traffic commercial areas

### 4. Pervious Concrete (CONC_POROUS)

**Description:** High-permeability concrete for stormwater infiltration.

**Specifications:**
- Thickness: 4 inches
- Porosity: 15%
- Infiltration: 480 in/hr (excellent)
- Compressive strength: 2,500 PSI (lower than standard PCC)

**Maintenance:** Similar to permeable asphalt - frequent sweeping critical

**Lifecycle Cost:** $20.00/sqft

**Sustainability:** 82/100 (excellent stormwater benefits)

### 5. Natural Stone (Bluestone) (STONE_NATURAL)

**Description:** Premium material used in historic and high-value districts.

**Specifications:**
- Material: Bluestone (slate) or granite
- Thickness: 1.5 inches
- Surface: Split face or honed
- Slip Resistance: 75 BPN (excellent)

**Maintenance Schedule:**
- Cleaning: As needed (pressure wash 500-800 PSI max)
- Repointing: Every 10-15 years
- Stone replacement: Individual stones as needed

**Lifecycle Cost:** $28.00/sqft (premium material)

**Benefits:**
- 50+ year lifespan typical
- Historic aesthetic appeal
- Very durable natural stone
- Common in Manhattan historic districts

### 6. Clay Brick Pavers (BRICK_CLAY)

**Description:** Traditional aesthetic, moderate maintenance, historic districts.

**Specifications:**
- Size: 3.625" x 7.625" x 2.25"
- Pattern: Running bond or herringbone
- Compressive strength: 3,000 PSI
- Slip Resistance: 68 BPN

**Maintenance Schedule:**
- Repointing: Every 5-7 years (Type S mortar)
- Cleaning: Periodic pressure washing
- Unit replacement: Individual bricks as needed

**Lifecycle Cost:** $20.00/sqft

### 7. Unit Pavers (PAVER_UNIT)

**Description:** Permeable cellular system, growing sustainability standard.

**Specifications:**
- Type: Open-cellular plastic grid
- Depth: 2 inches
- Infiltration: 600 in/hr (excellent)
- Open area: 60%

**Maintenance:**
- Vacuum sweeping: Every 3-4 months
- Fill replacement: Every 2-3 years
- Vegetation management: Quarterly if planted

**Lifecycle Cost:** $16.00/sqft

**Sustainability:** 75/100 (green infrastructure benefits)

### 8. Metal Surfaces (ADA Truncated Domes) (METAL_ADA_DOMES)

**Description:** Cast iron or integrated concrete warning surfaces at curb ramps and transit stations.

**Specifications:**
- Dome height: 0.5 inches
- Dome diameter: 0.9 inches
- Center-to-center spacing: 1.6 inches
- Slip Resistance: 65 BPN (must not be slippery)

**Maintenance:**
- Cleaning: Weekly (required for detectability)
- Rust treatment: Annual if iron
- Unit replacement: When domes wear smooth

**Lifecycle:** 15 years (cast iron requires more maintenance)

**Critical for ADA:** Detectable warning required at drop-offs (28 CFR 36.303)

## Surface Condition Assessment

Assessments use 5-point condition scale (ASTM D6433 Pavement Condition Index methodology):

| Condition | Deterioration | Description | Urgency |
|-----------|---------------|-------------|---------|
| EXCELLENT | 0-5% | Safe, accessible, minimal defects | Routine |
| GOOD | 6-15% | Minor defects, accessible, routine maintenance | Planned |
| FAIR | 16-30% | Accessible but degraded, planned maintenance required | Planned |
| POOR | 31-50% | Hazardous defects, urgent repair needed | Urgent |
| CRITICAL | 51%+ | Immediate repair required, ADA non-compliant | Emergency |

## Defect Types and Codes

### SP001-SP003: Spalling
- Concrete/asphalt loss and surface fragmentation
- **Severity Factors:** Depth, extent, edge stability
- **ADA Impact:** Trip hazard if > 0.5 inch

### CR001-CR004: Cracking
- Linear, alligator, or map cracking
- **Width measurements:** Hairline, fine, medium, wide
- **Treatment:** Seal cracks to prevent water infiltration

### PH001: Potholes
- Complete surface loss, bowl-shaped depression
- **Severity:** Depth > 1 inch typically hazardous
- **Remediation:** Full-depth repair to solid edges

### SE001-SE002: Settlement/Heave
- Uneven surfaces, vertical displacement
- **ADA Threshold:** > 0.5 inch is trip hazard
- **Cause:** Subsurface erosion, tree roots, poor base

### HV001: Tree Root Heave
- Uplifted surface from root growth
- **Common:** Manhattan parks, tree-lined streets
- **Remedy:** Root removal and surface repair

### RU001: Rutting
- Surface wear pattern, longitudinal depression
- **Traffic indicator:** High-traffic areas
- **Remedy:** Overlay or selective spot repairs

### ST001: Staining
- Discoloration, non-structural but affects appearance
- **Remedies:** Pressure washing, sealant treatment

### LE001: Loose Elements
- Pavers, tiles out of position
- **Priority:** Prevent complete failure

### RD001: Root Damage
- Subsurface and surface damage from tree roots
- **Challenge:** Complex remediation

### ADA001-ADA010: ADA Violations
- Accessibility non-compliance per 28 CFR 36
- **Categories:** Width, slope, slip resistance, firmness, protrusions

## ADA Compliance Rules

All materials must meet federal ADA requirements (28 CFR 36):

### ADA-1.2.1: Accessible Route Width
- **Requirement:** Minimum 4 feet clear of obstruction
- **NYC Impact:** Most common violation (narrow sidewalks)
- **Validation:** Measurement with tape

### ADA-1.2.2: Walking Surface Slope
- **Requirement:** Max 5% longitudinal, 2% cross-slope
- **Impact:** Wheelchair accessibility, mobility aid users
- **Validation:** Slope gauge measurement

### ADA-1.3.1: Slip Resistance
- **Requirement:** Minimum 60 BPN dry, 40 BPN wet
- **New surfaces:** 65 BPN minimum
- **Validation:** British Pendulum test (equipment)
- **Seasons:** Critical in winter (ice, snow)

### ADA-1.5.1: Firm and Stable Surfaces
- **Requirement:** No holes > 0.25 inch, no movement > 0.5 inch
- **Covers:** Potholes, cracked pavement, settlement
- **Impact:** Wheelchair users cannot navigate rough surfaces

### ADA-1.6.1: Changes in Level
- **Requirement:** Vertical ≤ 0.25" OK; 0.25-0.5" requires 1:2 bevel; > 0.5" requires curb ramp
- **Impact:** Prevents trip hazards

### ADA-1.7.1: Protruding Objects
- **Requirement:** Objects 4" into path, 27-80" above ground must be protected
- **Materials:** Primarily metal signs, grates

### ADA-1.8.1: Gratings and Openings
- **Requirement:** Openings ≤ 0.5" (perpendicular), ≤ 1.75" (parallel to travel)
- **Prevents:** Cane entrapment, wheel caster slip

### ADA-1.9.1: Detectable Warning (Truncated Domes)
- **Requirement:** 0.5" high, 0.9" diameter domes, 1.6" spacing, 24-36" depth strip
- **Locations:** Curb ramps, platform edges, transit stations
- **Critical:** Blind pedestrians depend on tactile detection

### NYC Local Law 60: Sidewalk Maintenance
- **Authority:** NYC Administrative Code § 34-814
- **Requirement:** Property owners maintain sidewalks free from hazards
- **Triggers:** 1" vertical displacement, potholes, > 10% cracking, missing material
- **Notice:** 14 days to remedy or DOT performs work and bills property

### NYC Winter Maintenance
- **Requirement:** Remove snow/ice within 4 hours (8am-4pm) or 8 hours (4pm-8am)
- **Standard:** Remove to bare pavement
- **Authority:** NYC Administrative Code § 34-805

## Maintenance Procedures by Material

### Asphalt Seal Coating
1. Clean surface with air blower
2. Apply hot asphalt emulsion (RS-2) at 0.25-0.35 gal/sy
3. Apply 100-200 lbs/sy cover aggregate
4. Cure 24-48 hours before opening to traffic
5. **Benefit:** Extends life 5-7 years, costs $0.40-0.60/sqft

### Concrete Joint Sealing
1. Clean joints to 1.5x width, 1 inch deep
2. Dry completely (critical)
3. Apply polyurethane or silicone sealant
4. Maintain seal every 5-7 years
5. **Benefit:** Prevents water infiltration, prevents spalling

### Pothole Repair (Full-Depth)
1. Remove failed material to solid edges
2. Prepare subgrade
3. Compact base
4. Tack coat pavement
5. Place new asphalt mix, compact to 96%
6. Temporary patch: Cold patch HMA (less durable)

### Crack Routing and Sealing
1. Route cracks to 0.5" wide, 0.75" deep
2. Clean with compressed air
3. Fill with rubber crack sealant or polyurethane
4. Prevents water infiltration and spalling

## Lifecycle Cost Analysis

**Total Cost of Ownership** includes installation, routine maintenance, preventive overlays, and eventual replacement.

| Material | Install Cost | Maintenance | Overlay | Replacement | Total Cost | Years |
|----------|--------------|-------------|---------|-------------|-----------|-------|
| Asphalt Standard | $2.50 | $0.40/yr×20 | $1.50 | $2.50 | $12.00 | 20 |
| Concrete | $4.50 | $0.10/yr×30 | $1.50 | $4.50 | $14.00 | 30 |
| Permeable Asphalt | $3.25 | $0.60/yr×20 | $1.50 | $3.25 | $18.50 | 20 |
| Bluestone | $15.00 | $0.20/yr×50 | $0 | $0 | $28.00 | 50+ |
| Brick | $8.50 | $0.30/yr×25 | $0 | $8.50 | $20.00 | 25 |

**Observation:** While asphalt has lowest upfront cost, concrete has lower total lifecycle cost due to longer life and less frequent maintenance.

## Sustainability Metrics

### Sustainability Score (0-100)

Factors considered:
- Recycled content (higher = better)
- Durability/lifespan (longer = more sustainable)
- Infiltration capability (permeable = green infrastructure benefit)
- Manufacturing carbon footprint
- Reusability/recyclability at end-of-life

**Scores by Material:**
- Recycled Rubber: 88/100 (100% waste diversion)
- Pervious Concrete: 82/100 (stormwater benefit)
- Permeable Asphalt: 78/100 (stormwater benefit)
- Bluestone: 70/100 (durable, long life)
- Asphalt Standard: 45/100 (petroleum-based)
- Concrete: 50/100 (energy-intensive cement)

### Carbon Footprint (kg CO2e per sqft)

Includes extraction, manufacturing, transportation, installation:
- Asphalt: 0.85 kg/sqft
- Concrete: 0.95 kg/sqft (cement production energy)
- Bluestone: 1.20 kg/sqft (quarrying and transport)
- Recycled Rubber: -0.10 kg/sqft (net negative - waste diversion)

## Integration with KPI System

Material taxonomy enables stratified performance analysis:

1. **Material-Specific Defect Rates**
   - Asphalt: Potholes, rutting, cracking
   - Concrete: Spalling, cracking, settlement
   - Permeable: Clogging, infiltration loss

2. **ADA Compliance by Material**
   - All materials must meet same width/slope/slip resistance
   - Specific rules by material (gratings for metal)

3. **Maintenance Cycle Adherence**
   - Asphalt: 3-year seal coat cycle
   - Concrete: 5-year joint sealing cycle
   - Permeable: 6-month to 2-year maintenance

4. **Lifecycle Cost Forecasting**
   - Aggregate total cost by material type
   - Project replacement needs
   - Budget multi-year renovation programs

## Glossary

- **BPN:** British Pendulum Number - measure of slip resistance (0-120 scale)
- **PCI:** Pavement Condition Index - standardized condition rating system
- **PSI:** Pounds per square inch - compression strength measurement
- **Infiltration:** Water permeability, measured in inches per hour
- **Lifecycle:** Total years of expected service life under normal conditions
- **Trip Hazard:** Vertical surface displacement > 0.5 inch
