# ADA Compliance Rules Reference

## Federal Standards (28 CFR 36)

This document maps ADA Accessibility Guidelines to NYC Street Design Manual implementation standards.

**Authority:** Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) of 1990, 28 Code of Federal Regulations Part 36

**Applicability:** All public sidewalks, pedestrian facilities, and pedestrian routes in NYC

---

## Core Accessibility Requirements

### 1. Accessible Routes (28 CFR 36.303)

**Rule ADA-1.2.1: Walking Surface Width**
- **Requirement:** Minimum 4 feet (1220mm) clear of obstructions
- **NYC Code:** Title 34 § 34-814 (sidewalk width standards)
- **Application:** All sidewalk materials equally
- **Enforcement:** NYC DOT; property owner liable under Local Law 60
- **Failure Severity:** HIGH
- **Testing Method:** Visual inspection with tape measure

**Rule ADA-1.2.2: Longitudinal Slope**
- **Requirement:** Maximum 5% grade (1:20 ratio)
- **Exception:** Where sidewalk must slope for drainage, use steepest necessary
- **NYC Implementation:** Match building frontage grade within this tolerance
- **Testing Method:** Slope gauge or transit level
- **Failure Severity:** MEDIUM (impacts wheelchair users)

**Rule ADA-1.2.3: Cross Slope**
- **Requirement:** Maximum 2% cross slope perpendicular to direction of travel
- **Purpose:** Prevents wheelchair roll-off
- **Measurement:** Use slope gauge perpendicular to travel direction
- **Failure Severity:** MEDIUM

---

### 2. Ground and Floor Surfaces (28 CFR 36.305)

**Rule ADA-1.3.1: Walking Surface Slip Resistance**
- **Requirement:** Minimum slip resistance coefficient
  - Dry: 60 BPN (British Pendulum Number)
  - Wet: 40 BPN minimum
  - New installations: 65 BPN minimum
- **Testing Method:** British Pendulum test (ASTM D3776)
- **Critical Seasons:** Winter (ice), summer (rain)
- **Materials Performance:**
  - Asphalt (rough): 65+ BPN (good)
  - Concrete (broom finish): 70+ BPN (excellent)
  - Asphalt (polished): 45 BPN (poor - below standard)
  - Bluestone (honed): 75+ BPN (excellent)
  - Metal gratings: Must be grooved or textured
- **Failure Severity:** HIGH
- **Remediation:** Resurfacing with textured finish or coating

**Rule ADA-1.5.1: Firm and Stable Walking Surfaces**
- **Requirement:** Surface must not:
  - Have holes > 0.25 inch (6mm) diameter
  - Allow movement > 0.5 inch (13mm) under wheel load
  - Buckle or shift under weight
- **Applies to:** Potholes, cracked pavement, heave, pavers out of alignment
- **Testing Method:** Visual inspection, straight edge for check
- **Failure Severity:** HIGH
- **NYC Implementation:** Local Law 60 triggers on 1 inch vertical displacement

**Rule ADA-1.6.1: Changes in Level**

Changes in level must be managed according to size:

| Height | Treatment | Slope | Validation |
|--------|-----------|-------|-----------|
| ≤ 0.25" | Vertical (no bevel) | Vertical | Visual |
| 0.25" - 0.5" | Beveled | 1:2 (50%) max | Measurement |
| > 0.5" | Curb ramp | 1:12 (8.3%) max | Design spec |

**Examples:**
- 0.75" step → Requires full curb ramp treatment
- 0.5" at street/sidewalk transition → Edge requires bevel
- 0.2" at building entrance → Allowable as vertical

---

### 3. Protruding Objects (28 CFR 36.307)

**Rule ADA-1.7.1: Protruding Objects (Overhang Hazards)**

**Requirement:** Objects protruding > 4 inches (100mm) into walking path, with leading edge between 27-80 inches (685-2030mm) above ground, must be protected.

**Purpose:** Blind pedestrians use cane detection (0-27" is detectable; >80" is above head)

**NYC Examples:**
- Street signs extending from poles
- Tree branches overhanging sidewalk
- Canopies/awnings over sidewalk
- Building trim or architectural features
- Traffic control boxes

**Remediation Options:**
1. Install guards below 27 inches to extend detection
2. Install warning strips or detectability features
3. Provide detectable curbing or edge
4. Relocate protruding object

**Testing Method:** Visual inspection with measurement

---

### 4. Gratings and Openings (28 CFR 36.302)

**Rule ADA-1.8.1: Grating Size Standards**

**Requirement:** Openings in surface gratings must not permit passage of a 0.5 inch sphere

**Specific Limits:**
- **Parallel to travel direction:** Max 1.75 inches (prevents cane entrapment)
- **Perpendicular to travel direction:** Max 0.5 inches (prevents wheel caster slip)

**NYC Applications:**
- Subway grates in sidewalks
- Utility access covers
- Tree grate openings
- Stormwater inlet grates
- Ventilation grates

**Testing Method:** Visual inspection; measure opening with calipers

**Failure Severity:** HIGH

---

### 5. Detectable Warnings (28 CFR 36.303)

**Rule ADA-1.9.1: Tactile Warning Surface (Truncated Domes)**

**Requirement:** Platform edges, curb ramps, and drop-offs must have detectable warning surface with truncated domes.

**Specifications:**
- **Dome Height:** 0.5 inches (13mm)
- **Dome Diameter:** 0.9 inches (23mm)
- **Dome Base Diameter:** 0.5 inches
- **Center-to-Center Spacing:** 1.6 inches (41mm)
- **Warning Depth:** Minimum 24 inches perpendicular to edge (36" preferred)
- **Visual Contrast:** High contrast to walking surface (e.g., yellow on dark)

**Locations (Mandatory):**
- All curb ramps at street/sidewalk transitions
- Transit station platform edges
- Escalator landings
- Stairs with drop-offs
- Reflecting pool edges
- Dock edges

**Materials:**
- Cast iron
- Concrete integrated (cast-in-place)
- Adhesive-applied rubber/plastic
- Truncated Dome Surface System (TDSS)

**Detectability:**
- Tactile: Cane users detect domes 12+ feet away
- Auditory: Cane creates distinct sound
- Visual: High contrast provides secondary warning

**Maintenance Critical:**
- Weekly cleaning to remove debris
- Monthly inspection for wear
- Replace when domes become smooth (non-detectable)
- Maintain visual contrast (repaint as needed)

**Failure Severity:** CRITICAL (accessibility requirement at hazardous locations)

## Material-Specific Rules

### Asphalt and Concrete

Apply all accessibility rules equally:
- ADA-1.2.1, 1.2.2, 1.2.3 (width, slope)
- ADA-1.3.1 (slip resistance)
- ADA-1.5.1 (firmness/stability)
- ADA-1.6.1 (changes in level)
- ADA-NYC-LOC-LAW-60 (maintenance)

**No Material Exceptions:** All must meet same accessibility standards

### Permeable Surfaces

Additional requirement: Maintain infiltration while meeting accessibility
- Cannot allow puddles > 6 inches (drainage)
- Must maintain slope for water management
- Cannot use ultra-slippery finishes

### Metal Surfaces (Gratings, Grates)

**Additional Rules:**
- ADA-1.7.1 (protruding objects from surrounding frame)
- ADA-1.8.1 (grating openings)
- ADA-1.9.1 (truncated domes on metal covers)

### Historic Materials (Bluestone, Brick)

**Standards Conflict:** Historic preservation may allow exemptions for:
- Maintaining historic material/pattern
- Preventing damage to irreplaceable material
- Technical infeasibility of retrofit

**NYC Practice:**
- Historic Preservation Commission (HPC) reviews exemptions
- Alternative accessibility requirements applied
- Must still comply with slip resistance and firmness

## NYC Local Regulations

### Local Law 60 (Sidewalk Maintenance)

**Enacted:** 2003, Effective: 2005

**Requirement:** Property owners maintain sidewalks in safe condition

**Triggers for Violation:**
1. **Vertical displacement:** > 1 inch
2. **Horizontal displacement:** > 1 inch
3. **Potholes:** Any pothole regardless of size
4. **Cracking:** > 10% of surface area cracked
5. **Missing material:** Any section missing material
6. **Heave/settlement:** Uneven surfaces affecting accessibility
7. **Loose elements:** Pavers, tiles out of position

**Enforcement:**
- NYC DOT issues violation notice
- Property owner has 14 days to remedy
- If not repaired, DOT performs work
- Owner is billed for repair costs
- Fines: $0-$500 per violation

**Example Cost Impact:**
- Sidewalk repair: $20-$150 per linear foot
- 20-foot frontage problem: $400-$3,000
- Professional remediation cost recovery

### NYC Administrative Code § 34-805 (Winter Maintenance)

**Snow and Ice Removal Requirement:**
- **Daytime (8am-4pm):** Remove within 4 hours
- **Nighttime (4pm-8am):** Remove within 8 hours
- **Standard:** Remove to bare pavement (no slippery patches)

**Why It Matters:**
- Creates temporary ADA violations during winter
- Increases slip hazard significantly
- Elderly and disabled most vulnerable
- Property owners fully liable for accidents

## ADA Categories of Violations

NYC DOT uses violation severity categories:

| Category | Examples | Response Time |
|----------|----------|----------------|
| CAT 1 (Critical/Emergency) | Impassable barrier, broken curb ramp, severe heave | 24 hours |
| CAT 2 (Urgent) | Multiple defects, cumulative accessibility loss | 7 days |
| CAT 3 (High Priority) | Significant defect affecting some users | 30 days |
| CAT 4 (Planned) | Minor defect, routine maintenance | 60-90 days |
| CAT 5 (Low Priority) | Cosmetic, staining, minor wear | 180 days |

## Compliance Checking Checklist

### Site Assessment
- [ ] Width: Measure walking surface, minimum 4 feet
- [ ] Longitudinal slope: Test with slope gauge, max 5%
- [ ] Cross slope: Perpendicular measurement, max 2%
- [ ] Firmness: Visual check for holes, test firmness with straight edge
- [ ] Changes in level: Measure all vertical transitions
- [ ] Slip resistance: British Pendulum test or visual assessment
- [ ] Protrusions: Measure overhangs, check for leading edge 27-80"
- [ ] Gratings: Measure openings (0.5" perpendicular, 1.75" parallel)
- [ ] Truncated domes: Check presence and condition at curb ramps

### Documentation
- [ ] Take measurements with calibrated tools
- [ ] Document with photos (show scale)
- [ ] Record GPS location
- [ ] Date and sign inspection report
- [ ] Keep records for legal defensibility

### Remediation
- [ ] Prioritize critical violations (CAT 1)
- [ ] Address high-severity ADA violations (CAT 2-3)
- [ ] Plan maintenance for routine defects
- [ ] Budget for material-specific care

## Federal vs. NYC Standards

| Issue | Federal (28 CFR 36) | NYC (Local Law 60) |
|-------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Width | 4 feet minimum | Varies by zone |
| Slope | 5% max | Same |
| Slip resistance | 60 BPN | Same |
| Vertical displacement | 0.5 inch max | 1 inch trigger for violation |
| Maintenance trigger | Material-dependent | Explicit defect list |
| Enforcement | HUD, private lawsuit | NYC DOT, property owner liability |

## Design Standards Integration

All material specifications in NYC Street Design Manual must meet these ADA minimums:

**Asphalt:**
- 65 BPN slip resistance (meets ADA with margin)
- 2" thickness minimum
- Proper base preparation for stability

**Concrete:**
- Broom finish for texture (70+ BPN)
- Joint sealing prevents tripping
- Proper grade and slope

**Natural Stone:**
- Honed finish acceptable but must test slip resistance
- Tight joints to prevent tripping
- Stable base essential

**Permeable Materials:**
- Must maintain drainage while keeping surface firm
- Cannot have gaps or separation
- Regular maintenance essential for both infiltration AND accessibility
